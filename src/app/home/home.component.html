Project Based
Learning(PBL)

The main advantage of project based learning is that it promotes problem-solving skills in real-world context. Project-based learning (PBL) is considered an alternative to the teacher-led classroom model. In fact, this will integrate theory and practice. The benefits are plenty including improved writing skill, understanding concepts, people and processes in an organization. The center thought of PBL is that real-world problems capture students’ interest and provoke serious thinking, as the students acquire and apply new knowledge in a problem-solving context

STEM Studio

Our Stem Labs provide a knowledge-centered learning environment to add value to the regular curriculum. The Lab focuses on Make it Yourself Project Based learning and is compliant with CBSE, NCERT and ICSE standards. The program duration is 40 weeks. The project-based learning methodologies followed at these labs enable students to be critical thinkers by:

  • Defining problems
  • Investing and generating possible solutions.

Atal Tinkering lab(ATL)

With the objective to promote entrepreneurship and innovation, the Government of India has rolled out its Atal Innovation Mission (AIM) which is setting up
knowledge hubs to shape young minds. These knowledge hubs are known as Atal Tinkering Labs (ATL) which provide a dedicated learning
environment to students of Class 6th to Class 12th. They learn innovation and skills,
and apply the same to develop ideas which will help transform India.
